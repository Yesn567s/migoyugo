Migoyugo Game Rules

The Game

  • Migoyugo is a board game for two players
  • It is an abstract strategy game that features complete information (no reliance on luck or chance)
  • Win by creating an Igo (an unbroken line of 4 Yugos) or have a higher Yugo count with a Wego

The Board

  • The Migoyugo board is an 8 X 8 grid of 64 squares (typically all the same color) with rows numbered 1-8 from bottom to top and columns designated A-H from left to right

The Migo

  • White always moves first by placing a piece, called a Migo, on any square on the board
  • Players take alternating turns placing Migos on any open square* (see No Long Lines)

The Yugo

  • When you form one unbroken line of exactly 4 pieces of your own color, or multiple intersecting lines (horizontal, vertical or diagonal), the last piece placed becomes a Yugo, represented by a dot in the center
  • When a Yugo is created, all Migos in the line(s) are removed, leaving behind only the Yugo created and any other Yugos in the line(s)
  • Yugos can never be moved or removed from the board

No Long Lines

  • At no time may either player create an unbroken line of more than 4 in a row of any combination of Migos and/or Yugos of their own color (multiple intersecting lines are permitted)

Winning

  • There are two distinct end game conditions – an Igo and a Wego
  • Form an unbroken line (horizontal, vertical or diagonal) of exactly 4 Yugos of your own color and you win instantly with an Igo
  • If on your turn, you are unable to make a legal move, or if all 64 squares are filled, the game ends with a Wego, and the player with the most Yugos is declared the winner. If both players have the same number of Yugos, the game is drawn
  • If a player resigns, the opponent is declared the winner
  • If players compete using a clock, a player is declared the winner if the opponent's clock expires
