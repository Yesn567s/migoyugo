Data Archives: Rules

01 // THE GAME

  • Migoyugo is a board game for two players.
  • It is an abstract strategy game that features complete information (no reliance on luck or chance).
  • Win by creating an Igo (an unbroken line of 4 Yugos) or have a higher Yugo count with a Wego.

02 // THE BOARD

  • The Migoyugo board is an 8 X 8 grid of 64 squares (typically all the same color).
  • Rows are numbered 1-8 from bottom to top.
  • Columns are designated A-H from left to right.

03 // PIECES: MIGO & YUGO

  • The Migo: White always moves first. Players take alternating turns placing a piece (Migo) on any open square.
  • The Yugo: When you form one unbroken line of exactly 4 pieces of your own color, the last piece placed becomes a Yugo, represented by a dot in the center.
  • When a Yugo is created, all other Migos in that line are removed.
  • Yugos can never be moved or removed from the board.

04 // RESTRICTION: NO LONG LINES

  • At no time may either player create an unbroken line of more than 4 in a row of any combination of Migos and/or Yugos of their own color.
  • Note: Multiple intersecting lines of 4 are permitted, but a single line of 5 or more is strictly illegal.

05 // WINNING CONDITIONS

  • THE IGO (Instant Win): Form an unbroken line (horizontal, vertical or diagonal) of exactly 4 Yugos of your own color.
  • THE WEGO (Count Win): If you are unable to make a legal move, or if all 64 squares are filled, the game ends. The player with the most Yugos wins. (Draw if tied).
  • If a player resigns, or their clock expires, the opponent is declared the winner.
